The Sentimental Propagation of Lottery Winnings: Evidence from the Spanish Christmas Lottery (with Evi Pappa and Isabel Mico-Milan)

We use the Spanish Christmas lottery as a ”natural” experiment to analyze the in- dividual and aggregate responses to a transitory income shock which is shared among many people living in the same province. Transitory income shocks induce significant de- mand effects that lead to a reduction in unemployment, a large rise in job vacancies and moderate increases in relative CPI prices at the province level. Drawing on individual survey data on sentiment and consumption, we show that households living in provinces awarded by the Spanish Christmas Lottery become more optimistic about their current and future economic conditions on impact and increase their durable consumption expen- diture significantly six months after the prize arrives, especially in furniture and vehicles. Lottery wins impact mostly the consumption and sentiment of younger individuals and have stronger effects during recessions.
