Economic Consequences of Public Investment News Shocks

This study constructs a new instrument for public investment news shocks based on narratives for European Investment Bank public loans. Using more than 6300 loans financing projects in 18 EU countries during 2000-2019, I show that the loans allocated to public firms are a good proxy for public investment news shocks. Using local projection estimation, I show that these shocks increase output, consumption, and employment in the economy without crowding out private investment. The cumulative three-years public investment multiplier multiplier is higher for countries with: lower per capita GDP, lower debt, lower uncertainty, higher local autonomy, and higher government size. Moreover, I study the effect of this shock on the EU firms investment using large scale firm-level data.
