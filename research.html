Who is Afraid of Sanctions? The Macroeconomic and Distributional Effects of the Sanctions Against Iran
The sanctions imposed on Iran at the beginning of 2012 have simultaneously limited the country’s access to the international financial system, levied a strict boycott on Iran’s oil and petrochemical exports, and limited imports of intermediate goods. This paper tries to quantify the aggregate and heterogeneous effects of these sanctions. Applying the synthetic control method, I show that the sanctions had persistent and significant effects on the Iranian economy. The cost reached its maximum of 19.1 percent of real GDP four years after the application of the sanctions, and the economy has not fully recovered after their removal. I trace the poverty dynamics for different household groups after the sanctions by adopting a synthetic panel using Iran’s household income and expenditure survey data. Inconsistently with the sanctions’ initial goals, poverty dynamics suggest that households working in governmental sectors and educated households are unaffected by the sanctions. Instead, the sanctions condemn young, illiterate, rural, or religious minority households to poverty
The Sentimental Propagation of Lottery Winnings: Evidence from the Spanish Christmas Lottery (with Evi Pappa and Isabel Mico-Milan)
We use the Spanish Christmas lottery as a ”natural” experiment to analyze the individual and aggregate responses to a transitory income shock which is shared among many people living in the same province. Lottery winnings spur economic sentiment and induce significant demand effects that lead to a reduction in unemployment, a large rise in job vacancies and moderate increases in CPI prices at the province level. Drawing on individual survey data, we show that households living in winning provinces, although they do not directly receive wins, become more optimistic about the current and future economic conditions on impact and increase their durable consumption expenditure significantly six months after the lottery draw, especially in furniture and vehicles. Lottery shocks impact mostly the consumption and sentiment of younger individuals and have stronger effects during recessions. We propose the Spanish Christmas lottery as an unconventional fiscal policy tool for increasing aggregate tax revenues and stimulating local economic conditions without crowding out aggregate activity
Monetary Policy and Firms R&D Investment Decision
This paper focuses on the effect of monetary policy on the research and development investment in the US economy. Using aggregate quarterly data and high-frequency monetary policy shocks, I show that a 25 basis point increase in the interest rate decreases the growth rate of aggregate $R\&D$ expenditure by 0.2 percentage points, and this effect persists for about five years. This effect holds mainly in the high-unemployment and/or lower financial uncertainty period. Furthermore, using firm-level data from Cumpostat for the time period 1990-2019, I show a similar result in the US firms' reaction to monetary policy shocks, and firms with higher liquidity, lower leverage ratio, and bigger firms increase their $R\&D$ investment more. A more careful disentangling of the monetary policy shock shows that this effect is mainly driven by federal fund rate shocks and forward guidance shocks
