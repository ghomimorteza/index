Monetary Policy and Firms R&D Investment Decision

This paper studies the effect of monetary policy shocks on the research and development investment in the US economy. Using aggregate quarterly data and high-frequency monetary policy shocks, I show that a 20 basis point increase in the interest rate decreases the growth rate of aggregate $R\&D$ expenditure by 0.18 percentage points, and this effect is persistent up to five years. Furthermore, using firm-level data from Cumpostat for the period 1990-2019, I show a similar result in the US firms' reaction to monetary policy shocks. Firms with higher liquidity and lower leverage ratio decrease their $R\&D$ investment more after a contractionary monetary policy shock. A more careful disentangling of the monetary policy shock shows that this effect on aggregate economy is mainly driven by federal fund rate shocks and forward guidance shocks.
